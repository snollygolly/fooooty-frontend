FOOOOTY RULES

  1. Introduction
    1. Fooooty is football/soccer simulation game, based on the 1980's PBM game 'UNITED' by Alan Parr. The sections of the rules taken from Alan Parr's 'UNITED' are marked (*) and are freely copyable, distributable, changeable, as long as no profit is made from the copy, and the orginal copyright is acknowledged to Alan Parr. Therefore those sections of the rules are copyright Alan Parr, but the code to implement the game, the look and feel of this web version, the artwork and all text not in those sections (such as the additional rules created by Fooooty Productions in order to implement this web version of the game), are copyright Fooooty Productions and may not be reproduced without written consent from Fooooty Productions.
    2. In Fooooty, we refer to the game as 'football', but our North American cousins will know the game as 'soccer'. When we say 'football' in Fooooty, we do not mean the game in which the oddly shaped, not-quite-a-ball is carried around in your hands rather than kicked with the foot. Fooooty is all about the game with the spherical ball kicked with the foot, in which David Beckham excels. No more will be said on the subject.
    3. In Fooooty, you take on the role of a football club manager, and have total control over coaching, team selection, tactics, buying and selling of players, and finances. Most divisions have 10 clubs, so you'll play 9 home and 9 away matches per season for a total of 18, plus some cup matches and maybe some friendlies too if you are so inclined. The season is created so that you play 2 league games per week, plus a week at the end for cup finals and tying up loose ends - this means a complete season lasts 10 weeks. There are places in the game where you can chat publicly to other managers, and generally have a good time outside of managing your club.

  2. Managerial conduct
    1. You are only allowed one account in Fooooty. We compare IP addresses to make sure that you are only managing one club, and we check email addresses so that no email address has more than one club associated with it. We also keep an eye on transfers and 'throwing matches', but please remember it ruins the game for other players if you break this rule.
    2. Offensive language and questionable conduct can also ruin the game for others, so please keep the swearing to a minimum, though some discretion is given (which is why there's a minimum age of 18 for playing Fooooty!). Bad language may be edited or removed by the admins, but only in extreme cases. Warnings will be given for continued bad behaviour, and as a last resort we will ban you from the game. The admins decision will be final in all matters, and no refunds will be given for any reason.
    3. Manually browsing to a specific Fooooty web page is not allowed. Editing the address bar while playing Fooooty is not allowed. Attempting to hack the game in any way is not allowed. Logging in as another user is not allowed. Disclosing your password to anyone (even admins) is not allowed. Posing as admins or moderators is not allowed. If you find a bug that affects gameplay, you must report it immediately. Breaking any of these rules is grounds for instant banning at the discretion of the admins.

  3. Your club
    1. Your club will have a name that you cannot change except at the start of a new season. (Chairmen can rename their club at any time - see below)
    2. A club name will initially have a suffix of 'United', e.g. if you name your club 'Westcliff' its full name by default will be 'Westcliff United'. (This is an homage to the original game United, on which Fooooty is based).
    3. You may change the suffix by spending Victory Points (VPs - see below).
    4. Your club will be located in a certain part of your home nation. Each nation is split into various halves:
      North/South, West/East, Inner/Outer, Urban/Rural, Town/Village.
      So a club could be, for example, located in North-East-Inner-Rural-Village. The location of a club determines which Regional Cups it can join (see below), and the cost of travel to away matches.

  4. Your players
    1. You are a manger in the game, so when we refer to 'players' we do not mean real human beings - we mean the fictional group of misfits that make up your club.
    2. Players in the game cannot be renamed. If you hold the rank of Chairman rather than Manager (see below), you are allowed to name new players as you discover them from your Youth Program.
    3. (*) All players have a native position on the field in which they play. There are six possible positions:
      1. Goalkeeper, abbreviated to GK
      2. Sweeper, abbreviated to SW
      3. Defender, abbreviated to DF
      4. Midfield, abbreviated to MF
      5. Forward, abbreviated to FW
      6. Utility, abbreviated to UT
      The DF + MF + FW areas are collectively known as the 'outfield'. Only UT players can play in any outfield area without disadvantage.
    4. (*) At the end of every season, each player ages by one and loses skill levels (see below) equal to his new age. A player begins his life as a schoolboy (SBY), and at the end of his first season becomes an apprentice (APP). At the end of the next season he becomes age I, then age II, then age III and so on until they retire. (Ages beyond APP are denoted in roman numerals). For example a player age II will become age III at the end of the season, and lose 3 skill levels. Youth players (SBY and APP) do not lose skills upon aging.
    5. (*) Players with a negative skill level will immediately retire, and cannot be brought back into the game. There is no upper limit to a players skill level. Skill levels are increased through coaching (see below).

  5. Choosing the squad
    1. From your list of available players, (your 'roster'), you must pick exactly 11 players to play your next match. These 11 players are known as your 'squad'. If you do not pick a squad before the match is played, the computer will randomly pick 11 players from your roster to become your squad for that one match.
    2. (*) There must be exactly one GK in your squad.
    3. (*) You have the option of playing one SW in your squad if you wish.
    4. (*) There must be at least 2 players in each of the outfield areas.
    5. (*) The area total (see below) of any outfield area cannot be more than 3 times that of any other outfield area. e.g. if the FW area total is 20, then neither DF nor MF could be more than 60 or less than 7. This restriction applies after all additions (see below) but before injuries and sendings off (see below) and has no effect on GK or SW. If your club somehow manages to break this rule, the extra levels will be ignored.
    6. After each match is played, your squad is cleared and must be re-selected before the next match. It is strongly advised therefore to log back in after each match and select the squad before your next match. If you are a Chairman, you have the option of retaining your squad after each game, making things a little easier for you.

  6. Playing a match
    1. (*) If your FW is greater than the opponents DF, you earn the number of shots equal to the difference.
    2. (*) If your MF is greater than the opponents MF, you earn the number of shots equal to half the difference rounded down.
    3. (*) If your DF is greater than the opponents FW, you earn the number of shots equal to a quarter of the difference rounded down.
    4. (*) All shots have to beat the opponents SW (if they are playing one), and then have to beat their GK.
    5. (*) If you have no shots from any area, 1 free shot will be awarded to your FW.
    6. (*) SW chance of stopping a shot is [30+(level x 2)]%
    7. (*) GK chance of stopping a shot is [40+(level x 2.25)]%
    8. (*) Any shot that beats the GK is a goal.
    9. (*) The club with the most goals wins. If the goals scored are equal, the match is a Draw unless it is a cup match in which case a penalty shootout will decide the winner.
    10. For league matches you are awarded 3 points for a Win, 1 point for a Draw, and no points for a loss. (Some leagues may change this point allocation in the future).
    11. For winning a cup match you are awarded 3 VPs, for a league win you get 2 VPs, for a league draw or a friendly win, you get 1 VP. You can exchange VPs for CPs or other in-game goodies.

  7. Adjustments to match levels
    1. (*) Home Advantage: The first named club in a match is designated to be playing at home (Home v Away). Assuming the home club is playing in their own stadium, they get an extra 7 levels to be split between the three outfield areas.
    2. If you employ a Groundsman (see below) you can increase your Home Advantage.
    3. (*) Great Performance Points (GPP). Each club has 30 GPPs to begin the season with. These can be assigned in any match, but cannot be carried over from one season to the next. They can be assigned to any outfield area with a maximum of 9 per match.
    4. (*) A club choosing to Play For a Draw (PFD) has 7 extra levels to split between SW, DF and MF, but its total shots will be halved (rounded down).
    5. (*) Provided a club does not field a SW, they can play the Off-Side Trap (OST). This halves the number of shots the opposing FW gets, but doubles the number of shots the opposing MF gets.
    6. (*) In each game, a manager can choose to add extra levels by playing hard. The number of extra points can be from 0 (weeds) to 9 (assassins), spread over the various areas as the manager decides. If a manager wished, he could play 9 hardness in every match. See the next section for the effects of hardness.
    7. (*) All the above affect AREA TOTALS, not individual player levels.
    8. (*) You can only apply extra levels to SW if you have a player in the SW area.
    9. (*) A maximum of 5 extra levels (from any mix of allowed sources) can be played on each of your GK and SW in a match.

  8. Player discipline
    1. (*) Players accrue Disciplinary Points (DPs) throughout the season.
    2. (*) Red/Yellow cards: For each player in a match there is a chance that he will be given a Yellow Card and booked (incurring 4 DPs) or given a Red Card and sent off (incurring 10 DPs).
    3. (*) Chance of being booked = [1.5 x (3 + your Hardness level for the match)]%
    4. (*) Chance of being sent off = [0.25 x (3 + your Hardness level for the match)]%
    5. Each time a player accrues DPs to reach a multiple of 10, they must sit out a number of matches. Only league and cup matches count - friendlies not played do not count as matches having been 'sat-out', nor can a player be selected to play in a friendly if they are banned.
    6. The number of matches to be sat out are as follows:
      1. 10 DPs = 1 match ban,
      2. 20 DPs = 3 match ban,
      3. 30 DPs = 5 match ban,
      4. 40 DPs = 7 match ban, etc...
      Note that it's possible for a player to have 18 DPs before a match, get booked and sent off during the match and therefore accrue 14 DPs thus putting him on 32 DPs. He would then have to sit out 8 matches; 3 for passing 20 DPs and 5 for passing 30 DPs. Playing hard is a short term fix for a long term problem!
    7. (*) There is a 10% chance of conceding a penalty for every level of hardness used. If no hardness is used there is still a 5% chance of conceding a penalty.
    8. (*) The chance of a penalty being scored is [50 + (4 x penalty takers skill) - (2 x GK skill)]% to a maximum of 95%
    9. (*) A player who is sent off will be deemed to operate at half their skill level for the entire match.
    10. (*) DPs are cumulative throughout the season.
    11. (*) At the end of the season, DPs are rounded down to the nearest 10, and then 20 DPs are subtracted (down to a minimum of 0, no negative DPs!).
    12. (*) Any players starting a season with 10 or more DPs will have to sit out the appropriate number of matches.

  9. Player Injuries
    1. (*) A major consequence of playing hard is that your opponents may sustain injuries.
    2. (*) For each of your opponents players the chance of them getting injured is [your total hardness played for that match + 2.5]%
    3. (*) The chance of getting injured is halved for a GK.
    4. (*) A player who is injured will be deemed to operate at half their skill level for the entire match. (One quarter if they are also sent off).
    5. (*) An injured player cannot be picked as a member of the squad.
    6. An uninjured player has an injury level of 0.
    7. Injuries caused are random, and go from 10 to 100. A club with a Physio (see below) can reduce the initial injury level.
    8. Every day a players injury level goes down between 1 and 10 points to a minimum of 0.
    9. A club with a Doctor (see below) can speed up the healing process.
    10. Each day that a player has an injury level of above 50, their skill level goes down by one.

  10. Playing Out Of Position (OOP)
    1. (*) GK cannot play OOP, and only GK can be assigned to play the GK position.
    2. (*) All other players can play OOP at 2 levels lower than normal.
    3. (*) UT players do not suffer from a 2 level handicap when playing at DF, MF or FW, but do play at 2 levels lower than normal when playing at SW.
    4. (*) If a player plays for 8 games OOP within a season, he is deemed to become a Utility player (UT).
    5. (*) Youth players (SBY and APP) cannot become UT by playing OOP, but still suffer the 2 level handicap when playing OOP.

  11. Club staff
    1. Scout: Normally you see only total shots on goal for matches in which you are not playing. If you assign a Scout to a match, you get a report telling you what each players levels are, and what adjustments each manager made (through GPP, PFD, OST, Hardness etc)
    2. Physio: By employing a Physio you reduce the amount of injuries sustained in a match.
    3. Doctor: By employing a Doctor you speed up the healing process for your players.
    4. Coach: By employing a Coach you decrease the cost of coaching your players to extra levels (see below).
    5. Groundsman: A Groundsman adds Home Advantage levels for when you play matches at home.
    6. Youth coach: Employing a Youth coach increases the levels of your new players when they graduate.

  12. Coaching
    1. (*) You spend Coaching Points (CPs) to increase a players level.
    2. (*) To raise a player by 1 skill level costs his new level in CPs. e.g. It costs 7 CPs to coach a player from skill level 6 to skill level 7.
    3. Employing a Coach decreases the cost of coaching a player.
    4. Clubs automatically gain 1 CP per day.
    5. You can buy CPs for your club by exchanging VPs.
    6. When a player is coached he cannot be coached again until after he plays in a match. This can be a league, cup or friendly match.

  13. Youth players
    1. (*) SBYs gain 1 skill level for every 3 matches they play.
    2. (*) At the end of a season, a SBY becomes an APP, and any spare matches are carried over to the next season.
    3. (*) APPs gain 1 skill level for every 4 matches they play.
    4. (*) At the end of a season, an APP becomes a level I player provided they are at least skill level 5, otherwise they fail to make the grade and leave the glamourous world of football to become IT consultants and are never heard from again.

  14. Transfers etc
    1. Transfer list:
      1. You can place any player (that is not up for auction) on the transfer list, but be warned that you cannot take a player off the transfer list.
      2. Choose a starting price for the player you wish to transfer. If no other club buys the player at that price then at the end of the day (i.e. midnight in your club's nation) the price will drop by 1% and the player remains on the transfer list. The 1% price drop happens every day until the player is bought.
      3. You cannot place an injured player on the transfer list, but a player can become injured while on the transfer list.
      4. You can continue to pick a player for the squad while they are on the transfer list, as long as they are not injured or banned.
      5. Beware that it is possible for a transfer-listed squad player of yours to be bought by another manager just minutes before your match is played. Unless you update the squad before the match is played, you'll be playing at a considerable disadvantage.
    2. Auction:
      1. You can put any player (that is not on the transfer list) up for auction.
      2. Choose the length of the auction and when the auction period is over the highest bid wins.
      3. Players up for auction who receive no bids are taken off the auction list when the auction period is over.
    3. Note that if you place a player on the transfer list, or put him up for auction, then certain attributes that are usually reserved for that player's manager's eyes only will become visible to everyone, such as wage, injury level, and other attributes usually shown as ? when you look at another club's player.
    4. Any player, for any transfer reason, that joins a nation that has a current league end date later than that of the nation he is leaving, will immediately age by 1 before arriving at his new club.
    5. You may fire any player that is not on the transfer list and is not up for auction, by agreeing to pay a penalty equal to the value of two weeks wages for that player. Fired players are retired, removed from the game and cannot be selected to play for any club at any time.
    6. (*) Any player that reaches a negative skill level for any reason (e.g. end of season skill level adjustment, or by sustaining an injury) is removed from the game and cannot be selected to play for any club at any time.

  15. Game Finances
    1. The currency in Fooooty is the 'k' and is global. All leagues use this currency.
    2. You start off with 1000k in the bank.
    3. You have ongoing weekly costs, such as player wages, staff wages, stadium upkeep, travel costs to away matches, etc.
    4. You can spend money on buying new players, purchasing upgrades to your stadium, staff and other aspects of the game.
    5. You gain income from gate receipts at home matches, winning cups and division championships, and by selling players.
    6. You can go into debt to a maximum of 1000k, but must pay a 1% daily interest on the sum you are in debt.
    7. If you go over 1000k in debt at the end of any day, you will be fired from your managerial position if you do not bring your club back under 1000k in debt within 7 days.

  16. League structure
    1. There are usually 10 clubs in each division, but some leagues may be created with more (or less) than 10 clubs.
    2. Generally, in each division, the top club will be promoted to the next division up. The bottom two clubs will be demoted to lower divisions. This may differ in some cases and will be explained when you view your division structure.
    3. Each club in a division is ranked by points, then by goal difference, then by goals scored, then by the previous days ranking.
    4. A full schedule of league matches is generated at the start of each season, but by mutual consent between two managers a match may be rescheduled to any free future date before the end of the season.

  17. Rescheduling matches
    1. In agreement with the manager of your opponent in a match, you can change the scheduled date of a match.
    2. You cannot reschedule a match to a date before your next match.
    3. You cannot reschedule a match to a date after the last match of the season.
    4. If you are participating in a cup (i.e. you have not been knocked out, and the cup is still waiting for someone to win it), you cannot reschedule a match to any day on which that cup's matches may potentially fall (see below).
    5. When you request a rescheduling with another manager, the potential new date is blocked off from use by you.
    6. If the other manager schedules a different match on the day you had requested, your request is immeditely denied.
    7. Some Botmanagers have been known to accept reschedule requests.

  18. Cups
    1. Each Fooooty nation holds a national cup to which all clubs within its borders are invited for no charge.
    2. Each Fooooty nation holds regional cups, and will charge a small entrance fee to participate.
    3. If you are a Chariman, you may set up your own cup by paying a fee of 100k plus a deposit which is used to pay the winner of the cup. You can charge an entrance fee to the cup, and decide which days of the week the cup is to be played. Whether you make a profit or loss organising the cup depends on the amount you set aside for the cup winner, how much you charge for entry to the cup, and how many clubs sign up to play in your cup.
    4. For any specific cup, all matches will be played on the same day of each week. (e.g. England may schedule all of its National Cup matches for Saturdays, whereas Canada may schedule all of its National Cup matches for Thursdays). These days are known as Cup Days, and can fall on different days for different cups.
      Possible cup schedule:
      Cup nameCup Day
      English National CupSaturday
      English Regional Cup (North)Tuesday
      English Regional Cup (West)Thursday
      Canadian National CupThursday
      Chairman's Cup
      (created by A.N.Other-Manager)      		Thursday
    5. You cannot sign up to a cup if any of your future matches fall on that cup's Cup Day. In the above example, English clubs can join the Chairman's Cup as long as they are not participating in England's Western Regional Cup, and have all their future Thursdays free of matches. Canadian clubs can also take part in the Chariman's Cup, as long as they have all their future Thursdays free of matches. This would prohibit any clubs still participating in the Canadian National Cup.
    6. You will be unable to schedule any other matches for a Cup Day until you are knocked out of that cup.
    7. You can reschdule cup matches to any future free day as long as you are bringing the date of the match forward.

  19. Friendlies
    1. You can arrange a Friendly match against any other human-managed club.
    2. You cannot arrange a Friendly for a date you already have a match scheduled, nor can you arrange a Friendly for a Cup Day if you are still in a cup that plays on that day.
    3. Players that are banned cannot participate in Friendly matches, and nor do Friendly matches count towards games having been sat-out for banning purposes.

  20. Account types
    1. Newbie account: All newcomers start off in Fooooty with a restricted set of menu items available to them. Once a series of actions are performed, which are designed to introduce new users to the interface and mechanics of the game, a Newbie will be awarded a Managerial account. It's possible to progress to a full-blown Managerial account within minutes, but it is suggested that you take time to get to know the game before you move on to the fully-fledged Managerial account.
    2. Managerial account: This is the basic account type for all users of Fooooty, and it shows that you've gone through the basics of most parts of the game, and understood how things work.
    3. Chairman account: This account type is available to all non-Newbie Managers. It costs 8 Credits per calendar month. Credits can be bought through Paypal, priced 100 credits for USD20.00/CAD20.00/GBP10.00/EUR15.00
